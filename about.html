About Darril Wilburn

A learner's journey through Toyota's teachings and beyond, discovering how timeless principles can serve leaders wherever they are.

Let me tell you a bit more about my journey.
My career started inside Toyota, where I spent over two decades learning what makes organizations truly excellent. Since then, I've had the privilege of working with leaders across industries, helping them discover that transformation isn't about implementing new tools, it's about changing how we think, how we lead, and how we develop the people around us.

From Toyota to Global Consulting

During my 20+ years with Toyota, I had the opportunity to work at three critical sites: Toyota's largest North American plant in Kentucky, the corporate headquarters, and the San Antonio, Texas plant start-up. I helped lead two of Toyota's most important cultural initiatives: The Toyota Way 2001, which codified Toyota's values of continuous improvement and respect for people, and Toyota Business Practice (TBP), a global problem-solving method where I facilitated the worldwide pilot and North American executive sessions.

Being trained directly by Toyota's internal sensei group (OMDD) gave me something invaluable: not just knowledge of lean tools, but an understanding of the thinking that shapes long-term excellence.

Consultant, Partner, and Guide for Leaders

Since leaving Toyota, I've partnered with organizations worldwide through honsha, niwaki, and other collaborations to guide leaders in building cultures that endure. My consulting goes beyond process improvement: it focuses on vision, alignment, and leadership courage.

I'm known for asking leaders the hard questions:

  • What future are you really leading your people toward?
  • Do you see the latent capability in your team, or only their current performance?
  • Are you managing results, or shaping the system that produces them?

By challenging assumptions and inspiring reflection, I help executives shape visions that lift entire organizations. My work blends the discipline of Toyota's principles with the adaptability leaders need in today's turbulent business landscape.

Speaker, Author, Teacher

As a keynote speaker, I've had the privilege of addressing global audiences, from the Harvard Business Review Latin America Series to Lean Netherlands. I've also written articles like Courage, Humility, Kaizen, I See Waste, It's Everywhere, and Myths and Paradoxes that Drive The Toyota Way.

I'm also a certified instructor of The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People, which allows me to bring timeless personal leadership principles into organizational transformation.

Core Belief

At the heart of my work is a conviction shared with Toyota's founders: every person has latent capability waiting to be unlocked. My mission is to help leaders believe in that potential, first in themselves, then in their teams, so they can shape organizations that are not only more successful, but also more humane, adaptive, and aligned with purpose.
