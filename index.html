From Teacher to Toyota Coach to Trusted Partner

My journey began in the classroom, teaching others how to learn. But it was at Toyota where I discovered that the greatest lessons come not from textbooks, but from walking the gemba—the real place where work happens.

Over two decades at Toyota, I helped design and implement the Toyota Way 2001 rollout, led the Toyota Business Practice globally, and witnessed firsthand how courage, humility, and continuous improvement transform not just processes, but people.

Today, as a consulting partner at Honsha, I guide leaders who want more than quick fixes. They want sustainable transformation rooted in principles that honor both performance and humanity.

Because true leadership isn't about having all the answers—it's about asking better questions and creating conditions where others can thrive.

Why Japan? Why Now?

The Toyota Way isn't just a business methodology—it's a philosophy deeply rooted in Japanese culture. To truly understand principles like continuous improvement, respect for people, and long-term thinking, you need to experience them in their cultural context.

Our Japan EDM isn't industrial tourism. It's a carefully curated experience that gives you exclusive access to Toyota suppliers, gemba walks with Japanese leaders, and cultural immersion that reveals why these principles work so powerfully.
